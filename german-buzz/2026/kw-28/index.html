KW 28 6 July – 12 July 2026

This week in Germany: summer heat, ice cream, office holidays and thunderstorms

These are practical topics you may hear in everyday conversations in Germany this week. The explanations and vocabulary below are designed for newcomers, expats, students, and international professionals.

Sommerhitze im Alltag

Talking about summer heat in Germany

In July, apartments and offices can become very warm. People often talk about opening windows early, finding shade, drinking enough water, and how difficult it is to sleep when the room stays hot.

  • die Hitze — heat
  • lüften — to air out a room
  • der Schatten — shade
  • Man merkt die Hitze heute wirklich. — You can really feel the heat today.

Eis essen gehen

Going out for ice cream

Ice cream shops are a common summer meeting point in Germany. Friends and colleagues may suggest getting an ice cream after work or ask which flavour you recommend.

  • die Eisdiele — ice cream shop
  • die Sorte — flavour or type
  • empfehlen — to recommend
  • Welche Sorte empfiehlst du? — Which flavour do you recommend?

Urlaubsvertretung im Büro

Covering work during the holiday season

Many colleagues take holidays during summer. Teams often discuss who will take over tasks, what still needs to be finished, and whether a short handover is required.

  • die Vertretung — cover or substitute
  • übernehmen — to take over
  • die Übergabe — handover
  • Kannst du mir kurz erklären, worum es geht? — Can you briefly explain what it is about?

Sommergewitter

Weather warnings and summer thunderstorms

After very hot days, thunderstorms are common. People check weather apps, talk about dark clouds, and change plans when strong rain or transport disruption is expected.

  • das Gewitter — thunderstorm
  • die Wetterwarnung — weather warning
  • betroffen sein — to be affected
  • Ich nehme lieber einen Regenschirm mit. — I would rather take an umbrella.
